# Life Engine

> A free, open-source personal data sovereignty platform. A self-hosted hub that gives you control over your data, your identity, and your digital life — with a plugin-driven native desktop client that anyone can use.

## What This Is

Life Engine combines two components into one unified product:

- **Core** — The self-hosted backend. A middleware layer that normalises all your external data (Google, email, calendar, identity documents, etc.) into a unified API *you* own. Your apps depend on *your* contract, not theirs.
- **App** — The client. A cross-platform application built with Tauri (Rust + JS/HTML/CSS) that starts as an empty shell and gains functionality entirely through plugins. You install only what you need. The client stays lean and personal.

Together they form a complete stack: Core manages your data, App gives you a UI to interact with it, and both are extensible through plugins.

## Why This Exists

Three forces are converging to make personal data sovereignty urgent:

- **Corporate surveillance** — Large companies profit from your data. You get the product; they keep the asset.
- **Data breaches** — Your data is scattered across dozens of services. Each one is an attack surface. When any of them gets hacked, you pay the price.
- **Forced identity verification** — Laws like Australia's Online Safety Act require platforms to collect government ID. You're handing your identity to companies you don't trust, stored in systems you can't audit.

People are already responding — avoiding services, using VPNs to circumvent age gates, abandoning onboarding flows. 68% of consumers have abandoned a digital onboarding process. VPN usage spikes 1,000%+ when verification laws take effect. The demand for an alternative is real.

Life Engine is a structural response: minimise the data you hand to third parties, own what you do share, and interact with your digital life through tools *you* control.

## Who This Is For

- **Anyone** who wants control over their personal data without needing technical skills
- **Privacy-conscious individuals** who are tired of handing data to corporations
- **Self-hosted enthusiasts** who want to own their digital infrastructure
- **Developers** who want to build on a stable, extensible personal data platform
- **The community** — this is free, open-source, and welcomes contribution

## Project Principles

- **Personal-first** — Built for individuals, not enterprises
- **Free and open-source** — No paid tiers, no freemium, no SaaS
- **Community-driven** — Open source, open governance, open contribution
- **Accessible** — Usable by people with zero technical skills
- **Universal** — Runs on any platform, depends on no vendor
- **Lean** — Install only what you need, nothing more
- **Owned** — Your data stays on your hardware, under your control
